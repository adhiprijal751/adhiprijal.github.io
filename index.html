About Adhip.

Hi, I’m Adhip Rijal. I'm a student at Williams College who’s passionate about designing tech that actually works for people. I love diving into projects that explore how we interact with digital spaces. My work is grounded in making technology more inclusive, intuitive, and meaningful. Blog & Projects

Here are some of the projects I did this semester for my HCI class.

20 Feb 2025

Exploring Apple Fitness: Strength and Weaknesses

This project was a needfinding study on Apple Fitness, aiming to evaluate its strengths and shortcomings in the health tech space. Through interviews with diverse users, I found that while the platform excels in usability, motivation, and Apple integration, it falls short in accessibility, accuracy for non-Watch users, and third-party app support—revealing both its potential and areas for growth.
18 Mar 2025

Incorporating Hand Gestures to Increase Accesibility

In this design sprint, our team reimagined the controls of the web-based game *Jewel Jet* by replacing keyboard inputs with intuitive hand gestures using MediaPipe's Gesture Recognizer. We selected the game for its simple mechanics and developed gesture controls through an iterative process of ideation, testing, and refinement, aiming to create a more fluid and accessible gameplay experience.
06 Mar 2025

Redesigning the Ephs Patient Portal

Our team redesigned Williams College’s Ephs Patient Portal to improve its usability and accessibility. By analyzing the current system and applying Human-Computer Interaction principles, we identified areas needing better navigation, readability, and user experience. We then created a modern, intuitive Figma prototype that streamlines access to health resources while aligning with the college’s branding.
30 Apr 2025

Analyzing Instagram’s Accesibility for People With Visual Disabilities

This project analyzes Instagram’s accessibility for users with visual impairments by combining firsthand user experiences with standards like the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG). By examining how screen readers, alt text, and contrast settings function on the platform, I identified key design barriers to equal access. The analysis reveals both Instagram’s strengths and its shortcomings, offering suggestions to make the platform more inclusive.
17 Apr 2025

Designing for Discoverability: A Bookstore UI/UX Makeover

In this design sprint for our human-computer interaction class, our team redesigned a basic book catalog using Bootstrap, focusing on refinement over reinvention. We identified usability issues, prototyped solutions, and implemented a more intuitive and user-friendly interface. This blog post reflects on our process and how small, intentional design choices can create a more human-centered bookstore experience.
12 May 2025

Seeing More Clearly: An AI-Powered Extension for Describing Online Images

Our team developed a lightweight browser extension that uses AI to generate image descriptions on demand, improving web accessibility for users with visual or cognitive impairments. In response to the widespread lack of alt text on the modern web, our tool helps make visual content more inclusive by providing quick, descriptive insights for anyone who needs or wants them.
